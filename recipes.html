Award-Winning Beef Chili

Author's Note: This super secret, never-been-shared recipe was the proud winner of the 2002 Grosse Pointe North/Grosse Pointe South Chili cookoff and has been seen by very few eyes. Consider yourself very lucky...


Ingredients

Quantity/Unit of Measure Ingredient
1/3 Cup Vegetable Oil
2 Cloves Chopped Garlic (or 1 tsp Crushed Garlic)
1 Large White Onion (Diced)
3 Lbs Ground Beef
2 - 28 oz Cans Petite Diced Tomatoes
1 - 46 oz Bottle Tomato Juice
1 - 16 oz can Pinto Beans
1 - 16 oz can Kidney Beans
2 Bell Peppers
3 Tbsp Chili Powder
1 Tbsp Cumin
1 tbsp Tabasco Sauce
1 tsp Frank's and/or Cholula Hot Sauce
1 tsp Crushed Red Peppers
Some Salt & Pepper

Summary

Heat vegetable oil in a large cooking pot over moderate heat. Add diced onions and garlic, saut√© until onions golden. Add ground beef, stir every 45-60 seconds until meat is browned. Remove pot from stove and strain contents until grease is gone. Return contents to pot, add tomato juice and both cans of diced tomatoes. Return pot to stove, add chili powder, cumin, dried red peppers, tabasco and frank's (or cholula) sauce. Stir contents and heat until boilin. Add bell peppers, turn to low heat and simmer with lid on for 2-3 hours (stir every half hour). 45 minutes before serving, rinse pinto and kidney beans in strainer and add to pot. Taste soup and add salt & pepper as needed.
